# Privacy

Noesis is a reading companion for the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy. It does not republish SEP entries, cache full articles, or scrape the SEP for redistribution. You read the original page on `plato.stanford.edu`; Noesis only stores the reading state and notes you create while reading.

You can use Noesis without an account. In that mode, saved entries, reading positions, highlights, and notes stay in your browser extension storage. If you clear the extension data, reset the browser profile, or uninstall the extension, that local data may be deleted.

Account sync is optional. If you sign in, Noesis uses Supabase Auth and stores the email address for your account, your Supabase user id, saved entry links, reading positions, highlights, and any notes you attach to those highlights. Highlight text is limited to the excerpts you choose to save.

Noesis does not sell personal data, run advertising tracking, or share your reading data with third-party marketing services.
