## Design Context

### Users
Noesis is for serious SEP readers: students, researchers, autodidacts, and philosophy-adjacent professionals who read long-form entries across sessions and need a lightweight way to preserve context.

### Use Cases
- Save canonical SEP entries without interrupting reading.
- Capture highlights and notes directly in the article.
- Resume from previous reading position.
- Sync local guest work into an account.
- Review saved entries and highlights in a quiet web library.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, scholarly, precise, modern.

### Aesthetic Direction
Clean and sleek without looking generic. Noesis should feel like a refined reading instrument: off-white paper surfaces, charcoal typography, restrained ink-like accents, clear hierarchy, and fast tactile interactions. It should not feel like a loud SaaS dashboard, neon AI tool, or generic card grid.

### Design Principles
1. Keep the reading flow calm and unobtrusive.
2. Use accent color sparingly for action and state, not decoration.
3. Prefer spatial hierarchy, typography, and subtle dividers over nested cards.
4. Design every state: empty, loading, error, success, disabled, hover, focus, active.
5. Make the extension feel instant; use optimistic UI where data loss risk is low.
